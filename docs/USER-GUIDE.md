# Farmore Wallet — User Guide

**Who this is for:** anyone who wants to send and receive crypto by name.
**Does it involve financial risk or running anything? No.** You don't run a node, you don't
bond anything, and you can't be slashed. You just send money by name. Your funds stay in
your control.

> 🛈 **The wallet isn't live yet.** Farmore mainnet and the wallet app are still being
> built. This guide describes how it will work.

## The idea

**One name. One balance. One account — across every blockchain.**

Instead of copying a 40-character address and worrying about which chain or which "gas
token" you need, you just type a name:

> Send 100 USDC to **@amara** → Send. Done in seconds.

Behind the scenes, the network's solvers deliver the money to Amara instantly on her chain.
You never pick a bridge, hold a gas token, or think about networks.

## What you get

- **One name (your handle).** Claim a handle like `@amara` once. People send to that name
  on any chain.
- **One balance.** The wallet shows your holdings across chains as a single total — money
  you can see and spend without tracking where it lives.
- **One account, self-custodial.** No seed phrase. Your account is secured by a **passkey**
  (your device's Face ID / fingerprint) and a **quorum of your devices** — a few of your
  devices together can sign, and if you lose one, you recover with the rest. Your keys stay
  yours; Farmore never holds your money.

## How you'll use it

1. **Get the app** and create your account with a passkey — no seed phrase to write down.
2. **Claim your handle** (first-come). That name is now yours across every chain.
3. **Receive:** share your handle. Anyone can send to `@you`.
4. **Send:** type the recipient's handle, the amount, and the asset, then confirm with your
   passkey. The recipient is paid in seconds on their chain.
5. **Sign in with your handle** to Farmore-enabled apps — proving you own the name, no new
   password.

## Good to know (honest limits)

- Delivery speed and the largest amount you can send depend on how much **solver liquidity**
  exists on the recipient's chain. Common chains and amounts are fast; **very large
  transfers, or transfers to chains with thin liquidity (especially Bitcoin), may be slower
  or temporarily unavailable** until the network grows.
- You pay a small fee for instant cross-chain delivery. Fees:
  **`[SET AT STAGE 2 — finalized on testnet before mainnet]`**.
- You don't manage gas, bridges, or networks — that's the solvers' job, not yours.
